# Pre-Publish Checklist

Every blog post on rovonnrussell.com runs this checklist before it goes live. Each item is a pass/fail. If anything fails, the post goes back to draft.

The point isn't to be precious. It's to make sure the work that gets published actually represents the brand. The tax of running this is twenty minutes. The cost of not running it is shipping AI-flavored content with Rovonn's name on it.

---

## Section 1 - Voice (8 gates)

The post must sound like Rovonn. Score 0â€“8. Need at least 7 to pass.

- [ ] **Opens with a specific moment, not a thesis.** First paragraph is observation. The thesis lands later. (If the first sentence tells you the punchline, fail.)
- [ ] **Names at least three specifics in the first half.** People, places, organizations, projects, tools, weeks. No generic "a client" / "some founders" / "many businesses" without a specific anchor nearby.
- [ ] **Follows observation -> reflection -> bigger picture at post level.** The structure is visible. You can label which section is which. (If the post jumps straight to bigger picture, fail.)
- [ ] **Uses at least one signature phrase naturally.** "What stood out most was...", "Walking away, I'm reminded that...", "Big respect to...", "What I loved most...". Not forced - used because it fits.
- [ ] **Has at least one "not X, but Y" contrast** (or earned tricolon). Not both in every paragraph. Used when it does real work.
- [ ] **Mixes sentence lengths.** Short punchy declarations next to longer reflective paragraphs. If every paragraph is the same length, fail.
- [ ] **Closes with credit and a forward-looking statement.** Names the people / partners / experiences the post draws from. Final sentence points at what's next. No hard CTA at the close.
- [ ] **Sounds like the cultural specificity is in the room when it should be.** If the post touches Toronto, Black community, organizing work, or impact partnerships - those things are named the way someone inside would name them. If the post is purely founder-craft, this gate is N/A.

## Section 2 - Forbidden patterns (hard gates)

Any one of these is an automatic fail. No score. Fix and re-run.

- [ ] **No em dashes anywhere in body copy.** Use commas, periods, or colons.
- [ ] **No "In today's fast-paced world..."** or any throat-clearing opener.
- [ ] **No "The most important lesson is this:"** or "The single biggest takeaway is..." patterns.
- [ ] **No formulaic listicle headers.** "5 Ways to X", "3 Mistakes I Made", "7 Tips for Y", "The Ultimate Guide to". Use sentence-style headers.
- [ ] **No "leverage" as a verb.** Use, draw on, apply, build on.
- [ ] **No generic AI-hype language.** "AI is transforming everything", "the future of work is here", "in this AI-powered world".
- [ ] **No rule-of-three for everything.** If the whole post is in threes (three mistakes, three lessons, three steps), the AI imposed that. Vary.
- [ ] **No "we" when it should be "I".** First person. "We" only for actual team moments.
- [ ] **No fabricated specifics.** Every named person, organization, place, or tool must be real or clearly anonymized. No invented anecdotes.
- [ ] **No copy-pasted phrasing from earlier posts.** Each post earns its own opener.

## Section 3 - Structure (5 gates)

- [ ] **Word count is 800â€“1,500.** Going under means the idea isn't earned. Going over means it should be split.
- [ ] **Has 3â€“5 anchor sections** with sentence-style H2 headers.
- [ ] **Frontmatter is complete.** title, slug, date, author, category, tags, excerpt, seoTitle, metaDescription, faq (4â€“8 items), published.
- [ ] **At least 4 FAQ items at the bottom** for on-page anchor linking and SEO. Plain-language questions someone would actually Google. Direct answers in voice.
- [ ] **Has a clear category** from the five: Building in Public, Storytelling, Systems & Agents, Community/Culture, ADAPT in Practice.

## Section 4 - Audience (3 gates)

- [ ] **Primary audience is identified** and the tone matches. Founders (peer-to-peer), Businesses (practical, ROI-aware), Impact-led leaders (serious, mission-respecting), or mixed (only when the principle holds for all three).
- [ ] **The reader earns the conclusion.** No "trust me, this works." The post walks the reader through enough specifics that they can decide for themselves.
- [ ] **The "so what" is clear by the end.** One sentence after reading: what changed for them.

## Section 5 - SEO and on-page (6 gates)

- [ ] **Primary keyword from `SEO_KEYWORD_MAP.md` is targeted.** Appears in title, H1, first 100 words, and at least one H2 naturally.
- [ ] **Meta description is under 160 characters** and reads like a sentence, not a stuffing of keywords.
- [ ] **SEO title is under 60 characters** and front-loads the primary keyword.
- [ ] **At least 2 internal links** to other posts, pillar pages, or core site pages (Visibility Starter Kit, ADAPT, Work With Me).
- [ ] **At least 1 outbound link** to a credible source when claims need backing (research, partner orgs, public data). Avoid linking to competitors.
- [ ] **Hero image is set** and has descriptive alt text. Not generic stock. Either real photo, branded graphic, or pulled from project work.

## Section 6 - Distribution-ready (3 gates)

- [ ] **The lede paragraph could survive as a LinkedIn post on its own.** Strong observation, specific, hooks attention. (The `rr-distribute` skill will use this.)
- [ ] **At least one paragraph in the post is quotable on its own.** A line that would land as a tweet or pull-quote.
- [ ] **The reader knows what to do next.** Forward-looking close points at the kit, ADAPT page, Work With Me, or another related post - naturally, not as a sales pitch.

## Section 7 - Final read-through (the gut-check gate)

- [ ] **Read it aloud.** If anything makes you wince, mark it. Fix or cut.
- [ ] **Would Rovonn say this on a call?** If a sentence feels written for SEO or for an algorithm, rewrite it for a real reader.
- [ ] **Does the post earn its publish slot?** If the answer is "I'm just publishing to publish" - hold it. The calendar can absorb a quiet week.

If a post fails any hard gate (Section 2), the entire post fails. If it scores below 7/8 on voice, it fails. If it misses any single gate in Sections 3â€“7, it fails.

Most posts will fail on first run. That's the point. The first-run failures usually concentrate in: forbidden patterns leaking back in, the close turning into a CTA, or the post skipping the moment and jumping to the thesis. Fix and re-run.

When a post passes the full checklist, it's ready to publish. After publish, hand to `rr-distribute`.
