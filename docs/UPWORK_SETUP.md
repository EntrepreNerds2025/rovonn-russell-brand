## Connects strategy

Upwork's Connects (the bidding currency) are limited. Spend them surgically.

**Filter every job by:**
- Payment method verified ✓ (skip unverified clients — they default at 4x the rate)
- 5-star rating, 5+ jobs posted, $5K+ total spent
- Hire rate > 50%
- Posted within last 24 hours (older jobs are usually filled or stalled)

**Skip every job that:**
- Says "looking for cheapest" or lists a budget under $500
- Asks for unpaid sample work
- Has 50+ proposals already
- Is from a brand-new client with zero history

**Spend per proposal:**
- Use the boost feature only on jobs you'd genuinely take. Boosting doubles your visibility but doubles the Connect cost.
- Aim for 4-6 boosted proposals per week to high-fit jobs, not 30 default proposals to mediocre ones.

**Proposal template (customize each time, but starts the same):**
```
Hi [name],

I noticed [specific detail from the job post that shows you read it]. Sounds like you're trying to [restate the core problem].

I've shipped [N relevant agents/systems/posts] in this exact lane. The pattern that usually works for [their problem type] is [one-sentence architectural insight].

Before I quote, I'd want a 20-30 min call to make sure I'm scoping the right thing. Free, no commitment. If after the call we both think it's a fit, I'll send a written proposal with timeline and price.

A few proof points:
- [Link to portfolio sample 1]
- [Link to portfolio sample 2]
- [Link to your blog post most relevant to their domain]

Available [your earliest call window]. Want to grab a slot?

Rovonn
```

This template wins because it's specific (you read the post), confident (you've done this before), and flips the dynamic (free call before quote). Most freelance proposals come in cold with a price quote attached, which signals desperation.

## Off-platform exit strategy

Upwork takes 10-20% of every transaction depending on lifetime billing. After you've worked with a client for 6-12 months and earned the relationship, it's standard practice to invoice them directly off-platform on the next engagement. Read Upwork's TOS — there's a "Conversion Fee" if you do this within the first two years of working together. Plan for it.

Long-term: use Upwork as a lead source for the first 2-3 clients in each service line, get the case studies and testimonials, then route future buyers off-platform through your own site (rovonnrussell.com/work-with-me).
