## Caption template

Same voice as the blog and LinkedIn. Three layers: observation -> reflection -> bigger picture. Always close with credit + forward-looking.

Length:
- Quote cards: 1-2 short paragraphs in the caption
- Photo with overlay: 3-5 paragraphs (mini blog post)
- Framework cards: 2-4 paragraphs explaining what's on the card and why it matters
- Carousels: short caption (the carousel does the work) + 1 paragraph of context + clear CTA to the link in bio

Hashtags: 3-7 max, end of caption. Mix branded (#StorytellingAndSystems, #ADAPTFramework) and audience-finding (#FoundersJourney, #ImpactLeaders, #PracticalAI).

## What to NOT do (anti-patterns)

- **Don't use generic stock photos.** Even one stock photo in the grid telegraphs that you don't have real material to work with. Better to have fewer posts than fake ones.
- **Don't post Reels of just text-on-video for the first month.** Static carousel posts and photo-with-overlay are doing more work right now while you build the visual library.
- **Don't try to nail nine perfect posts on day one.** Three posts a week for three weeks gets you there. Better to publish at a sustainable rhythm than burn out chasing the mockup.
- **Don't cross-promote impactloop.ca in your captions.** Mention "the studio I founded" if relevant, but the personal feed leads people to the personal site. Cross-references stay subtle.
- **Don't follow-back random accounts to grow.** Insta's algorithm in 2026 cares about engagement quality far more than follower count. A 1,000-follower account where every post gets 50 saves outperforms a 10,000-follower account where every post gets 30 likes.

## What to do (the simple growth playbook)

1. Post 3x a week on a fixed schedule (Tuesday / Thursday / Saturday is a good baseline).
2. Reuse blog post material - every blog post can produce 1 quote card, 1 carousel, and 1 photo-with-overlay.
3. Engage with 10 accounts in your audience every day before posting your own content. Substantive comments, not "great post." This is how Insta's algorithm learns who your audience is.
4. Use the Saves metric as your north star. Saves signal high-value content. They're the strongest engagement signal Insta tracks for personal brands.
5. Update the highlights every 2-3 weeks as new content goes up. Old highlights look stale fast.
