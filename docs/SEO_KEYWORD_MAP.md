# SEO Keyword Map - rovonnrussell.com/blog

The keyword strategy across the five blog categories. Each cluster has primary keywords (target rank), secondary keywords (support and internal linking), and search-intent notes.

This isn't a keyword-stuffing playbook. The goal is to write posts that genuinely serve the reader for a specific search, then make sure the post is structured so Google and AI search engines can read it. The pre-publish checklist enforces the on-page side. This doc supplies the targets.

## Strategic posture

**Subdirectory, not subdomain.** The blog lives at rovonnrussell.com/blog so domain authority compounds in one place. 301 redirects from blog.rovonnrussell.com forward link equity from any prior posts.

**Topical clusters, not isolated posts.** Each category has a pillar page on the main site (eventually) and posts cluster around it. Internal links flow up to the pillar and across to sibling posts in the same category.

**AI search optimization.** Every post has FAQ blocks at the bottom. Frontmatter is structured. Sentence-style headers double as direct answers to queries. Posts answer one clear question with specifics.

**Voice over volume.** No rapid-fire publishing to chase rankings. The voice doc is the moat. AI-flavored content from competitors will rank for a while and then fall as Google's helpful-content signals catch up.

## What we're not doing

- No keyword stuffing. The voice rejects it and Google detects it.
- No mass-publishing thin posts to chase ranking. The voice doc is the moat.
- No competing on volume against larger publishers. Compete on specificity, lived experience, and the cultural / operator combination only Rovonn has.
- No clickbait headlines. Sentence-style headers, true to the post.

## Quarterly review

Every quarter, pull the top 5 ranking posts and the bottom 5. Look at what's working: which keywords are converting to email signups (Visibility Starter Kit), which are converting to consulting inquiries, which are pure traffic. Update the keyword targets for the next 12 posts based on what's learned. The map is a living document.
