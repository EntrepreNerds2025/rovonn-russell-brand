# Editorial Calendar — rovonnrussell.com/blog

24 posts mapped across five categories. Each row gets a working title, the primary audience, the search-intent keyword cluster, and a one-line angle hook in the voice. The calendar is meant to be cycled through — when a post ships, the next one in its category becomes the working draft.

Every post follows: observation → reflection → bigger picture. Every post names specifics. Every post closes with credit and a forward-looking line. See `VOICE_REFERENCE.md` and `PRE_PUBLISH_CHECKLIST.md`.

## Categories at a glance

- **Building in Public** — the operator's view from inside Impact Loop, Nerds Creative, and the personal brand. What's actually shipping, what's broken, what changed.
- **Storytelling** — the craft: narrative architecture, story capture, writing for visibility, how messages land.
- **Systems & Agents** — practical AI and workflow systems for founders and SMB operators. What works, what doesn't, the math behind the choice.
- **Community / Culture** — Toronto, Black community organizing, youth programs, the conditions that make community work actually work.
- **ADAPT in Practice** — the framework applied to real cycles. Each phase (Assess, Discover, Apply, Produce, Transform) shown through specific moments. The deliverable across these case studies varies — sometimes a content engine, sometimes an agent, sometimes brand work, often a hybrid. The point is to show the *tailoring*, not a single output type.

## Cadence

Two posts per week is the long-term rhythm — one anchor post (Building in Public, Systems & Agents, or ADAPT in Practice) on Tuesday, one shorter post (Storytelling or Community/Culture) on Friday. At 24 posts, that's a 12-week runway. Ship one, queue the next.

## How to use this calendar

When starting a new post:

1. Pick the next post in the category that's been quiet longest. Don't bunch posts in the same category back-to-back unless one is breaking news.
2. Read `VOICE_REFERENCE.md` before drafting. Especially the cultural specificity and audience translation sections.
3. Hand the row above to `rr-blog-writer` (the skill). It loads voice + checklist + this row and produces an MDX draft.
4. Run `PRE_PUBLISH_CHECKLIST.md` against the draft before publishing.
5. After publish, hand to `rr-distribute` for LinkedIn / X / newsletter / Pinterest versions.

When the post is shipped, mark it with the publish date in this file and add a one-line note on what landed.

## Future expansion

When a post in the calendar gets shipped, replace it with a new post idea sourced from one of:
- A real moment Rovonn captured in the weekly story-capture habit (preferred).
- A reader question that came up more than twice.
- A thing a founder or impact leader specifically asked for during a strategy call.

The calendar is a queue, not a fixed plan. Posts that stop feeling true should be retired without ceremony.
